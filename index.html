💊CCB / BB Overdose
Key Agents
  • High-dose insulin (HDI): 1 unit/kg bolus → infusion
  • Calcium gluconate or chloride IV
  • Atropine (bradycardia)
  • Vasopressors (norepinephrine, vasopressin)
  • Lipid emulsion therapy (ILE)
  • Consider glucagon (BB OD)
  • ECMO / IABP for refractory shock

💊Acetaminophen Overdose
NAC Protocol
  • IV NAC (Acetadote®): 150 mg/kg over 1 h → 12.5 mg/kg/h × 4 h → 6.25 mg/kg/h × 16 h
  • Plot on Rumack-Matthew nomogram
  • Stop when: APAP undetectable + AST/ALT improving + no prognostic markers
  • Extend if: elevated aminotransferases, elevated INR, persistent APAP

💊Opioid Overdose
  • Naloxone 0.4–2 mg IV/IM/IN; repeat q2–3 min PRN
  • Infusion: 2/3 of effective bolus/hr if repeated dosing needed
  • Airway support, O₂, suction
  • Monitor for re-sedation (extended-release opioids)
  • Avoid over-reversal → precipitates withdrawal & agitation

💊Valproic Acid Overdose
  • Supportive care (airway, hemodynamics)
  • L-Carnitine 100 mg/kg IV (max 3 g) q8h if encephalopathy / liver toxicity
  • Activated charcoal if early and airway intact
  • Hemodialysis for massive OD or hemodynamic instability
  • Monitor ammonia, LFTs, coags

🍶Toxic Alcohol Poisoning
Methanol / Ethylene Glycol
  • Fomepizole (4-MP): 15 mg/kg IV load → 10 mg/kg q12h × 4 doses → 15 mg/kg q12h
  • Ethanol (alternative): target level 100–150 mg/dL
  • Folate (methanol) / Pyridoxine + Thiamine (ethylene glycol)
  • Hemodialysis: severe acidosis, organ failure, high levels
  • Correct metabolic acidosis

⚡Sodium Channel Blocker OD
e.g. TCAs, cocaine, flecainide
  • Sodium bicarbonate 1–2 mEq/kg IV bolus → infusion (target pH 7.45–7.55)
  • Goal: narrow QRS, reverse hypotension
  • Avoid flumazenil if TCA suspected
  • Lipid emulsion for severe refractory toxicity
  • Manage seizures with benzodiazepines

Bicarb in Na-channel OD
🧂Hyponatremia
  • Severe symptomatic: 3% NaCl 150 mL IV over 20 min (repeat × 2 PRN)
  • Target: raise Na⁺ by 5 mmol/L in first hour
  • Max correction: ≤8–10 mmol/L per 24h (risk of osmotic demyelination)
  • Identify cause: SIADH, hypovolemia, hypervolemia
  • Tolvaptan / vaptans: SIADH (specialist use)
Expert Panel Recs (2014)
🦴Hypercalcemia of Malignancy
  • IV fluids: 0.9% NaCl 200–500 mL/hr (volume expansion)
  • Zoledronic acid 4 mg IV over 15 min (preferred bisphosphonate)
  • Denosumab 120 mg SC for bisphosphonate-refractory or CKD
  • Calcitonin 4–8 units/kg q6–12h (rapid onset, short-lived)
  • Dialysis for severe/refractory with renal failure
Hypercalcemia of Malignancy Review
⚗️Hyperkalemia
  • Calcium gluconate 1 g IV (membrane stabilization — give first if ECG changes)
  • Insulin 10 units + Dextrose 50 g IV (shift K⁺ intracellular)
  • Salbutamol 10–20 mg nebulized (additive shift)
  • Sodium bicarbonate (if acidosis)
  • Kayexalate / patiromer / sodium zirconium cyclosilicate (elimination)
  • Dialysis if refractory or anuric
NEJM Hyperkalemia Review
🦋Hyperthyroidism / Thyroid Storm
  • PTU 500–1000 mg load → 250 mg q4h (preferred in storm; blocks T4→T3)
  • Methimazole 60–80 mg/day (alternative)
  • Iodine (Lugol's) 5–10 drops TID — give ≥1h after PTU
  • Propranolol 60–80 mg q4h PO or IV (rate/symptom control)
  • Dexamethasone 2 mg q6h (blocks T4→T3 conversion)
  • ICU admission for storm (Burch-Wartofsky score ≥45)
ATA Guidelines
🌡️Myxedema Coma
  • Levothyroxine (T4): 200–400 mcg IV load → 50–100 mcg/day IV
  • Liothyronine (T3): 5–20 mcg IV q8h (add if severe or no response)
  • Hydrocortisone 100 mg IV q8h (until adrenal insufficiency excluded)
  • Passive rewarming, supportive care, ICU
  • Avoid rapid correction → can precipitate arrhythmias
ATA Guidelines
🩸DKA / HHS (Adult)
  • IV fluids: 0.9% NaCl 1 L/hr × 1–2 h, then adjust to clinical status
  • Insulin: 0.1 units/kg/h infusion (hold until K⁺ ≥3.5 mmol/L)
  • Potassium: replace aggressively (target 4–5 mmol/L)
  • Glucose: add D5W when BG <14 mmol/L to continue insulin
  • DKA resolved: pH >7.30, HCO₃ >15, anion gap ≤12
  • Transition to SC insulin 30 min before stopping infusion
Diabetes Canada CPG DKA Medical Protocol
👶Paediatric DKA
  • Resuscitation bolus: 0.9% NaCl 20 mL/kg over 30 min (repeat if needed)
  • Rehydration: even correction over 36h per weight-based rate
  • Insulin: 0.05–0.1 units/kg/hr — NOT before K⁺ ≥3.5 mmol/L
  • Cerebral edema signs → 3% NaCl 5 mL/kg IV over 10 min / mannitol 0.5–1 g/kg
  • NO IV insulin bolus in children
TREKK Peds DKA BCCH DKA Protocol
📉Ventricular Arrhythmias
  • Pulseless VT/VF: Defibrillate → ACLS → amiodarone 300 mg IV
  • Stable VT: Amiodarone 150 mg IV over 10 min → 1 mg/min × 6h → 0.5 mg/min
  • Procainamide 15–17 mg/kg IV load at 25–50 mg/min → infusion
  • Correct electrolytes (K⁺ >4.0, Mg²⁺ >0.9 mmol/L)
  • Torsades de Pointes: Mg²⁺ 2 g IV, stop QT-prolonging drugs
  • Electrical storm: amiodarone + beta blocker + call cardiology
2022 ESC VA Guidelines
💓Atrial Arrhythmias
AF / A-Flutter
  • Unstable → DC cardioversion immediately
  • Rate control: metoprolol 2.5–5 mg IV q5 min; diltiazem 0.25 mg/kg IV
  • Rhythm control (acute): amiodarone, ibutilide, or electrical cardioversion
  • AF <48h: cardioversion generally safe; >48h needs anticoagulation or TEE
  • Anticoagulate per CHA₂DS₂-VASc score
2020 ESC AF Guidelines
🌿Digoxin Toxicity
  • Digoxin-specific antibody fragments (DigiFab): indicated for K⁺ >5 mmol/L, haemodynamic instability, life-threatening arrhythmias
  • Acute OD: # vials = (mg ingested × 0.8) / 0.5, or empiric 10–20 vials
  • Chronic toxicity: 3–6 vials
  • Avoid calcium IV (may worsen toxicity)
  • Atropine for bradycardia; transient pacing if needed

🔄DOAC Reversal — DTI (Dabigatran)
  • Idarucizumab (Praxbind) 5 g IV (2 × 2.5 g vials) — complete reversal
  • Indications: life-threatening bleeding, urgent surgery
  • Effect immediate and sustained ~24h
  • Hemodialysis removes dabigatran if antidote unavailable
  • Can restart anticoagulation as soon as clinically appropriate

🔄DOAC Reversal — Anti-Xa
Apixaban, Edoxaban, Rivaroxaban
  • Andexanet alfa (Ondexxya): low dose 400 mg IV bolus → 480 mg infusion; high dose 800 mg → 960 mg (rivaroxaban >10 mg or apixaban >5 mg)
  • 4F-PCC (Octaplex/Beriplex) 25–50 units/kg IV: alternative if andexanet unavailable
  • Tranexamic acid adjunct for severe bleeding
  • Avoid FFP alone — insufficient anti-Xa reversal

🧠Status Epilepticus
  • 0–5 min: ABC, O₂, IV access, glucose check; thiamine 100 mg IV if at risk
  • 5–20 min (Phase 1): Lorazepam 0.1 mg/kg IV (max 4 mg) or diazepam 0.15 mg/kg IV; midazolam 0.2 mg/kg IM if no IV access
  • 20–40 min (Phase 2): Fosphenytoin 20 PE/kg IV, or valproic acid 40 mg/kg IV, or levetiracetam 60 mg/kg IV (all max 4500 mg)
  • >40 min (Phase 3): Anaesthetic doses — propofol, midazolam, or pentobarbital infusion; ICU, EEG monitoring
AES/Epilepsy Foundation 2016
😮Angioedema
Allergic vs HAE vs ACEi-induced
  • Allergic: Epinephrine 0.3–0.5 mg IM q5–15 min; antihistamines + steroids
  • HAE (C1-INH deficiency): C1-INH concentrate (Berinert) 20 units/kg IV; or icatibant 30 mg SC; or ecallantide 30 mg SC
  • ACEi-induced: Stop ACEi; icatibant 30 mg SC; FFP 2 units if refractory
  • Airway management is priority — intubate early if progressive
  • Antihistamines/steroids NOT effective in HAE
WAO HAE Guidelines
🌡️Malignant Hyperthermia
  • Dantrolene 2.5 mg/kg IV bolus → repeat 1 mg/kg q5–10 min (max 10 mg/kg); then 1 mg/kg q6h × 24–48h
  • Stop triggering agents (volatile anaesthetics, succinylcholine) immediately
  • Active cooling (target <38.5°C)
  • Correct acidosis, hyperkalemia, rhabdomyolysis
  • Call MHAUS hotline: 1-800-986-4287
MHAUS Treatment Protocol
🫁Acute Pulmonary Embolism
Risk-Stratified Approach
  • High-risk (shock/arrest): Systemic thrombolysis — alteplase 100 mg IV over 2h (or 0.6 mg/kg over 15 min in arrest)
  • Intermediate-high: Anticoagulation + monitor; consider PERT activation
  • Heparin: 80 units/kg bolus → 18 units/kg/hr (therapeutic range aPTT 60–100 s)
  • Catheter-directed thrombolysis or surgical embolectomy for refractory
2019 ESC PE Guidelines
🫀Acute Valvular Heart Disease (VHD)
Acute AR / Acute MR
  • Vasodilators: nitroprusside, nitroglycerin (reduce afterload)
  • Inotropes: dobutamine / dopamine if hypotensive
  • IABP contraindicated in AR; may be used in MR as bridge
  • Urgent surgery: definitive for acute AR & primary acute MR
Acute AS / Acute MS
  • AS: avoid aggressive vasodilation; volume cautiously; urgent TAVI/SAVR
  • MS: rate control (beta blocker / CCB), diuresis, urgent BMV if severe
  • All acute VHD → early Heart Team discussion
  • MCS as bridge to definitive intervention if haemodynamically unstable
